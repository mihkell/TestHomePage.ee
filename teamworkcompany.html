New Model of Company Arhitecture in IT services

This business model is meant for companies that rent workforce or provide software development outsourcing. The aim is to fix the compensation model currently prevailing in the industry.

With currently prevailing business models workers and owners are at odds with one another. Owners have incentive to reduce wages to the minimum, and workers have incentive to do as little work as possible.

In many business sectors the capital investment of owners is significant but starting an IT services company requires little starting capital. However the model how owners are compensated is the same.

When we start IT services company it's very clear with what the owners are risking. Their risk is equal to the amount of unpaid work they are willing to invest before they decide that they failed or they succeeded.

The capital needed to invest by the owners is very quantifiable therefore we don't need to pay them a perpetuity. We should instead pay a reasonable amount for the work that starting a company requires. After the members of the company have compensated the founders then the owners shouldn't get any more money for the fact that they are the de facto owners of it.

The model's basic statment is that in a company we should compensate work. The money left after paying compensations should be considered as "market fluctuations"/"market opportunity benefit" and divided between workers.

All the things that need to be done in a company should be defined as work. This creates a situation where somebody who wants the benefits/salary of their team member is only required to be able to perform those tasks.

The Model

  1. *Compensate activities that benefit company - idealy you compensate only/mostly work. (Starting a company is work also. I'd suggest to set a fixed number how much this costs. E.g. founders risk with 2 month of compensation plus they work extra hours that need to be compensated with 10x. When company succeeds then the new workers will have to pay that sum back to owners, over some period.)
  2. Compenstaion should be fixed sum and not perpetuity
  3. People in a team get the income of the project. E.g. If project managers negotiate an increase of $/h the benefits go to the team members.
* - Seniority is also compensatable.
